<h4>1. Objectives of the PE Programme</h4>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>to acquire a range of motor skills, concepts, principles and strategies
to participate in 6 different sports</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>to display positive personal and social behaviour across different experiences
and environments</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>to acquire health, diet, fitness and sports knowledge for lifelong commitment
to a physically active and healthy lifestyle</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>to build a resilient character with an appreciation of the outdoors</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h4>3. Modes of Assessment</h4>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>NAPFA test&nbsp; (PFT)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Theory assessment through SLS and HBL</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Game skills assessment through peer feedback and intra-class competitions.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>4. Department Policy</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>All students, except those with valid medical certificates, are to participate
fully in PE lessons.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All students are to be in their House T-shirts, school PE shorts, socks
and appropriate footwear for all PE lessons.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All students are to participate in sports events organised by the department.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p>5. Sports and Exercise Science (ESS) as an O-Level subject</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>The choice of taking ESS as an O-Level subject is offered to Secondary
3 students who are more sport-inclined, provided they meet the requirements
at the end of the Secondary 2 year.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>There is no N-Level paper for ESS. N(A) students will be taking their
ESS O-Level papers concurrently with their N-Level Papers.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>The 2-year curriculum will enable students to appreciate the theoretical
and practical aspects of PE as well as prepare students who are keen in
a tertiary education in:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Life and Health Sciences</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Physical Education</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Health and Fitness</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
