<p><strong>Objectives of NFS Programme</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>To develop students’ understanding of the concepts of nutrition and meal
planning</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To develop students’ understanding of the link between diet and health</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To develop students’ understanding of the principles of food science</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To equip students with the knowledge and skills to make informed decisions
concerning food and nutrition</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>NFS Core Skills Programme</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Skills: Culinary skills and Methods of Cooking</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Vocabulary: Common terminology used</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Concept: Diet, Health and Food Management</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Mode of Assessment</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Written Test</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Practical</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Coursework</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>NFS as a Subject</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Lower Secondary: a compulsory subject</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Upper Secondary: Elective as an O-Level and N-Level subject</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The syllabuses, which include content and coursework components, are taught
over two to three years.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The coursework component develops students’ ability to apply a range of
process skills, such as the ability to apply and synthesize knowledge and
evaluate the outcome of a completed task.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>NFS Signature Programmes</strong>
</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Secondary 1 Inter-Disciplinary Coursework (Food Photography), collaboration
between NFS and Art Department</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Secondary 2 Food Science Coursework (Investigative Experiment)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Lower Secondary Culinary Enrichment, conducted termly by NFS teachers</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Secondary 3 Culinary Enrichment, conducted by external vendor</p>
</li>
